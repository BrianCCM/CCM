## Executive Summary

Cedar City Landscaping (the trade-name front for ALL GREEN Landscape Management LLC) is a long-tenured, owner-operated landscaping outfit that has been in business since 2007. Despite ~19 years of operation in a small market (Cedar City, UT, population ~38k), the business is being out-marketed at every level by newer entrants. The website is functionally broken on the modern web: **the HTTPS version returns `ERR_TLS_CERT_ALTNAME_INVALID`**, meaning every visitor who clicks a Google result, types the URL with `https://`, or follows a link from a directory listing gets a full-screen browser security warning before ever seeing the site. Most never click through.

The HTTP version of the site is bare-bones — a single-page brochure listing services (lawn maintenance, sprinkler repair, fertilizing, weed spraying, snow plowing) without any meaningful trust building, social proof, conversion paths, or local SEO infrastructure. Meanwhile, the company's reputation is fractured across review platforms: a 1.0-star rating on Yelp, ~3.6 on Birdeye (9 reviews), and ~3.9 on Google My Business — none of which would be acceptable to a discerning customer comparison-shopping against competitors.

By contrast, the dominant local competitor — Everything Exterior — has 400+ Google reviews, a polished modern site with city-specific landing pages, was named 2019 Cedar City Small Business of the Year, and ranks for nearly every commercial keyword in the market. National franchisee Augusta Lawn Care has also planted a flag locally with a professional, conversion-optimized site. Cedar City Landscaping is, in marketing terms, invisible to every prospect who does even five minutes of comparison shopping.

This is a high-fit prospect for Cedar City Marketing: a local, established, owner-operated business with budget capacity, a broken website, weak reputation infrastructure, and aggressive competition that's actively eating their lunch. The HTTPS cert failure alone is a one-call sales hook.

## Quick Wins (0–30 days, low cost / high impact)

1. **Fix the HTTPS certificate** — Issue a free Let's Encrypt cert (via the host) so `https://www.cedarcitylandscaping.com` and `https://cedarcitylandscaping.com` both resolve cleanly with a valid SAN that includes both apex and `www`. This is the #1 priority. Right now Google flags this site as "Not Secure," Chrome blocks it with a full-page warning, and any customer pasting the URL into iMessage / Facebook / a text gets a broken preview. **Estimated cost: $0–50. Estimated lift: 30–60% in click-through-to-site rate.**
2. **Force HTTPS redirect + canonicalize** — After the cert is in place, redirect all HTTP traffic to HTTPS and pick a canonical host (`www` or apex, not both).
3. **Claim and complete the Google Business Profile** — Address the ~3.9 rating with a review-response strategy and a request-flow to current happy customers. Even moving from 3.9 to 4.5 would dramatically improve map-pack click-share.
4. **Address the 1.0-star Yelp rating publicly** — A 1.0 on Yelp is a deal-killer for any prospect doing comparison shopping. Either reply to the negative reviews professionally, or aggressively solicit new ones from satisfied customers to dilute the rating.
5. **Add a phone CTA above the fold** — The current site buries contact info. The phone (435-531-1032) should be a tap-to-call button in the header on mobile.
6. **Add a "Request a Quote" form** — Single-step form: name, phone, service needed, address. No form on the current site = every visitor must call, which kills conversion from after-hours visitors.
7. **Add real photos and a basic services grid** — Replace text-only descriptions with photos of recent work and a clean 6-service grid (Lawn Care, Sprinkler Repair, Fertilizing, Weed Control, Snow Removal, Landscape Maintenance).
8. **Add a "Why ALL GREEN" trust block** — "Family-owned in Cedar City since 2007," "We answer the phone," "Free estimates" — basic credibility content the site currently lacks.

## Strategic Recommendations (1–6 months)

1. **Full website rebuild** — Single-page site is insufficient. Build a proper site with: home, individual service pages (lawn care, sprinkler repair, fertilizing, weed control, snow plow), gallery, about, contact, blog. Each service page is a separate SEO target.
2. **Local SEO foundation** — Add LocalBusiness schema, NAP consistency across the top 25 citation directories, optimized Google Business Profile with weekly posts and Q&A, embedded Google Map on contact page.
3. **Reputation management system** — Automated post-job review request via SMS to push happy customers to Google. Goal: 50+ Google reviews at 4.6+ stars within 6 months.
4. **City + service landing pages** — Mirror Everything Exterior's playbook: pages for "Lawn Care Cedar City UT," "Sprinkler Repair Enoch UT," "Snow Plowing Parowan UT," etc. There are 6–8 surrounding towns this business could rank for.
5. **Resolve naming confusion** — The legal name (ALL GREEN Landscape Management) and trade name (Cedar City Landscaping) and domain (cedarcitylandscaping.com) and Yelp listing (All Green Landscape Management) are all inconsistent. This fractures reviews, citations, and brand recognition. Pick one customer-facing name and consolidate.
6. **Paid search test** — Cedar City has cheap CPCs. $500–800/mo on Google Ads targeting "sprinkler repair Cedar City" and "snow plowing Cedar City" with proper landing pages would likely produce 5–15 qualified leads/month at a low CPL.
7. **Content engine for off-season pages** — Blog content on Cedar City–specific topics (best grass for high-altitude desert, Iron County watering restrictions, when to winterize sprinklers in southern Utah). Modest investment but compounds for years.

## Long-Term Initiatives (6–24 months)

1. **Brand consolidation and positioning** — Decide whether the brand is "ALL GREEN" or "Cedar City Landscaping," then unify domain, GBP, social, signage, vehicle wraps, invoices.
2. **Service productization** — Move from quote-by-quote to packaged tiers (Bronze/Silver/Gold annual maintenance contracts). Recurring revenue is much higher LTV than transactional.
3. **Geographic expansion** — Add St. George / Hurricane / Parowan service area pages and dedicated landing pages.
4. **Referral / loyalty program** — Built into the website with a trackable referral link.
5. **Video content** — Before/after job videos for YouTube + GBP. Drives organic discovery and trust simultaneously.

## Detailed Analysis by Category

### 1. Content & Messaging — 32/100

**Findings.** The current site is a single page with thin, generic copy. The page title that surfaces in Google is a keyword-stuffed run-on: "Cedar City Lawn Care, Lawn Maintenance, Sprinkler Repair, Fertilizing, Weed Spraying, Snow Plowing." There is no clear headline, no value proposition, no positioning statement, no story. The copy that does exist is functional ("we offer commercial and residential snow plowing and ice control") but reads like a directory listing, not a brand. There's no mention of the 19 years of local tenure — a massive missed credibility lever. There is no owner photo, no team page, no testimonials section, no gallery, no FAQ. Service descriptions are one or two sentences each.

**What's missing.** Headline that names the customer's problem; subhead that names the outcome; trust microcopy (years, reviews, guarantees); social proof (Google review count and rating displayed prominently); service-specific subpages; before/after photography; FAQ; team/about content; clear primary CTA.

**Why this score.** This is brochure-grade content from ~2010. The competition is shipping fully built-out content marketing.

### 2. Conversion Optimization — 22/100

**Findings.** The site fails at the most basic conversion fundamentals:

- **Broken HTTPS** is a conversion catastrophe. Modern Chrome, Safari, and Firefox display a full-screen "Your connection is not private" interstitial that requires the user to click "Advanced" → "Proceed anyway" to even see the site. Industry data puts the bounce rate on these warnings above 95%. Effectively, every HTTPS visitor is lost.
- No quote form anywhere on the site
- No tap-to-call button optimized for mobile
- Phone number is not prominently styled or placed in the header
- No urgency triggers, no offer, no incentive to act
- No live chat, no SMS path, no Calendly-style booking
- Forms (if any) likely don't have clear, single-action design
- No exit-intent capture, no remarketing pixel, no email capture

**What it should look like.** Header with logo + phone CTA + "Get Free Quote" button. Hero with headline, sub, hero image, primary CTA, secondary CTA. Trust strip directly below hero (review count, years, license). Lead form on every page with 4 fields max.

**Why this score.** The HTTPS issue alone is worth ~30 points off. Combine with absence of fundamentals and 22 is generous.

### 3. SEO & Discoverability — 28/100

**Findings.**

- **Broken HTTPS = direct Google ranking penalty.** Google has used HTTPS as a ranking signal since 2014. Sites without valid certs are de-prioritized in results and flagged in Search Console.
- **No HTTPS = no modern browser features.** No service worker, no geolocation, no clipboard API. PageSpeed and Core Web Vitals are likely degraded as a result.
- **Single-page architecture** means there's effectively one URL to rank — instead of 10–30 service- and city-specific pages competing on long-tail keywords.
- **No schema markup** (LocalBusiness, Service, Review, FAQPage). Competitors are using rich snippets and getting bigger SERP real estate.
- **NAP inconsistency** across Yelp, Google, Birdeye, Yahoo Local, Facebook, etc. (different names, sometimes different addresses).
- **Title tag is keyword-stuffed**, suggesting late-2000s SEO playbook. Modern Google penalizes this.
- **No content engine** — no blog, no recent updates. Google's freshness signals are dead.
- **Domain age** is the only positive — `cedarcitylandscaping.com` has been registered for many years, which provides a weak baseline.

**Competitive reality.** Searching "lawn care Cedar City UT" returns Everything Exterior, Cedar Lawn Care, Augusta Lawn Care, and various aggregators (Yelp, Angi, Thumbtack) on page 1. Cedar City Landscaping does not appear in the map pack or organic top 5 for most commercial queries despite owning a near-perfect exact-match domain. That's a damning indictment of the on-page execution.

**Why this score.** Strong domain name and 19 years of indexed presence prevent this from being a 10. Everything else is failing.

### 4. Competitive Positioning — 30/100

The Cedar City landscaping market has 4 clear archetypes:

| Competitor | Reviews / Rating | Site Quality | Positioning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Everything Exterior** | 400+ Google reviews; 4.8 / 508 reviews on Chamber of Commerce; 96% recommend on Facebook (131); 5-star Yellow Pages; 2019 Cedar City Small Business of the Year | Modern, multi-page, city + service landing pages, blog, polished | Premium, "honest local top-rated," all-in-one (lawn + windows + Christmas lights) |
| **Cedar Lawn Care** | 3.8 on Angi; family-owned 15+ years | Functional, mid-tier site | Family-owned local, broad service mix |
| **Augusta Lawn Care of Cedar City** | New (FB shows 0 reviews); franchise | Polished national franchise template; multiple city + service pages | National brand operating-system applied locally |
| **ALL GREEN / Cedar City Landscaping** | 3.9 Google; 1.0 Yelp; 3.6 Birdeye (9) | Single-page, broken HTTPS | Unclear — competing on price/longevity but not communicating either |

**Diagnosis.** This is a 19-year incumbent being lapped by both a polished local market leader (Everything Exterior) and a new national franchise (Augusta). They have the longest local tenure of any of the four but they are not telling that story, and their digital presence makes them look like the smallest / least credible option even though they're not.

**Strategic gap.** No defensible positioning. Could realistically own one of: "Cedar City's longest-serving landscaper" (true since 2007), "The owner-answered phone" (true if Eric still picks up), or "Snow + lawn — one company year-round" (true and differentiated from Augusta). None of these are being communicated.

**Why this score.** Real assets exist (tenure, year-round service mix, owner-operated). They are entirely unleveraged.

### 5. Brand & Trust — 35/100

**Trust signals present.** Phone number on site; physical address findable via directories; 19-year track record (not communicated); some positive Birdeye/Google reviews referencing owner Eric by name.

**Trust signals missing or actively damaging.**

- **Broken HTTPS** is the single most visible trust failure online. A "Not Secure" badge in the URL bar tells every customer: *this business doesn't pay attention to detail*. For a service that requires letting strangers onto their property and into their backyard, that's an extinction-level perception problem.
- **1.0 star Yelp rating.** Even one or two angry Yelpers crater the rating, but for any prospect who checks Yelp first this is fatal.
- **No reviews on the website itself.** No widget, no testimonial section, no Google embed.
- **No team photos, no owner photo, no truck photo.** The business is faceless.
- **No license number, insurance proof, BBB badge, or industry association badges** displayed.
- **Inconsistent business naming** (Cedar City Landscaping vs. ALL GREEN Landscape Management vs. ALL GREEN Landscape Maintenance) erodes credibility.

**Why this score.** The owner Eric clearly delivers good work (positive reviews on Birdeye and Google reference his Sunday emergency calls and reasonable pricing). The marketing fails to translate that reality into perceived trust.

### 6. Growth & Strategy — 80/100

**Why this is the only strong dimension.** The business itself has excellent bones:

- 19 years of operation in a stable market — survival is signal
- Owner-operated stability with tenure
- Year-round revenue mix (lawn season + snow season smooths cash flow)
- Owns the exact-match domain (cedarcitylandscaping.com)
- Cedar City is a small enough market that a competent marketing operator can move the needle quickly
- Local CPCs are cheap, so paid acquisition is viable
- Established customer base provides email-list and referral upside if mined

The growth potential is high *because* the marketing floor is so low. The business is profitable enough to have survived 19 years on word-of-mouth alone — modern marketing applied to that foundation should produce step-function growth.
