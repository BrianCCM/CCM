Lead Marketing Strategist

Brian Johnson

Brian has been in marketing since 2019, building his craft through years of freelance work that touched every corner of the discipline. Managing ad campaigns, designing websites, developing social strategies, and running analytics for real clients taught him fast what actually moves the needle. Since 2023, he's brought that same framework to the insurance industry, helping multimillion-dollar agencies sharpen their marketing operations.

His philosophy is simple: marketing should make money. Brian doesn't get attached to any particular channel or tactic. He's run dozens of Google Ads and Meta accounts, built social strategies that drove hundreds of thousands of dollars in revenue, and designed custom websites for clients across industries. If a campaign is ROI-positive, it keeps running. If it isn't, he works on it until it is.

Brian and his family have called Cedar City home for more than five years. He grew up spending a lot of time in Southern Utah, and while he never planned to put down roots here, he's genuinely grateful for the people and community he's found. Working with Cedar City Marketing means working with someone who has a real stake in this place.

Content & Paid Ads Specialist

Madi Vikari

Madi joined the Cedar City Marketing team with a marketing degree from Southern Utah University and hands-on experience most recent grads don't have yet. While finishing her B.S. in Marketing at SUU, she was already managing real social media content for the SUU Business School and co-leading the university's student engagement marketing team, developing campaigns that reached hundreds of students across Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn.

Her focus at Cedar City Marketing spans content creation, social strategy, and paid advertising. She knows the platforms from the inside out and understands that great creative has to do more than look good. Whether she's building out a content calendar or dialing in ad targeting, she's always thinking about performance.

Before marketing full-time, Madi spent over a year in competitive retail sales, ranking in the top 15% company-wide out of more than 70 employees and generating over $200,000 in revenue. That background shapes how she approaches everything: she thinks about marketing through the lens of the customer and the result, not just the click.
